Screen-by-screen

Detailed flow

Each card describes one screen's purpose, the inputs/state it owns, and the transitions in and out of it. Cohort comparison is the product's center of gravity — every main-app screen either shows cohort context or lets the user pivot to Comparison.

2 · Demographics

The most important onboarding page per client. Everything downstream (cohort filters, percentile math, drug rankings, price comparisons) pivots on these fields.

Required
Age · Sex · City + State · Height (ft+in) · Starting weight.
Optional
Race / Ethnicity (multi-select chips; "Prefer not to say" is exclusive).
In / Out
In: Welcome · Back → /. Out: Continue → /medication.

Captures the 1–5 starting point the app tracks against over time. Enables "Baseline → now" shifts on Dashboard and Insights.

Main app · /dashboard (hub)

6 · Dashboard

The landing hub after onboarding. Every path either starts here or returns here.

Header band
Lōkahi logo on the left · bell (→ /notifications) + avatar (→ /profile) on the right · "TRIAL WEAVE · GLP-1" eyebrow + greeting underneath.
Blocks
Current regimen (Days / Weight Δ / Next dose) · Cohort CTA ("top 28%…" → /comparison) · Adherence ring (tap → /adherence) with cohort median delta · Quick actions × 4 · Baseline → now grid.
Main app · /comparison

7 · Comparison (cohort hub)

The client's favorite surface. Answers the two product questions: "How do I compare to people like me?" and "What drug works best for people like me?"

Structure
Two sub-tabs directly beneath the header. CohortBadge with filter chips + n= sits below.
Sub-tabs
Your results (default) · Better for you?
Sub-tab · /comparison (your-results)

7a · Your results

Head-to-head comparison of the user against matched cohort medians.

Outcomes (6)
Weight change (12 wk) · Adherence rate · Side-effect days / mo · Energy · Mood · Avg monthly cost.
Per row
You vs Cohort median · Better / Below · percentile bar with midline tick.
Sub-tab · /comparison (better-for-you)

7b · Better for you?

Ranks all GLP-1 drugs by outcomes within the user's demographic cohort.

Hero
One-sentence data-driven recommendation + informational disclaimer.
Ranked list
7 drugs numbered 1–7; current drug highlighted. Per row: rank · brand + generic · 3 stats (Weight Δ, Side fx, Rating) · tag (CURRENTBest efficacyBest tolerabilityBest balance).
Main app · /insights

8 · Insights

Cohort-first analytics. "People like you" framing leads every block.

Blocks
Cohort hero ("On Mounjaro, your cohort loses 18.2% in 12 wks") · GLP-1 outcomes within cohort · Side effects by drug · Monthly cost by drug · Your shifts since baseline · Side-effect trend · Export.
Side effects by drug
4 drugs each show their top 3 side effects as cohort-incidence bars. The current drug is highlighted and annotated with the user's personal 90-day counts ("you: 12 in 90d").
Monthly cost by drug
All 7 GLP-1s with cohort median as a grey bar; the user's drug gets a teal tick mark at their actual spend (e.g., median $62, you $45).
Main app · /adherence

9 · Adherence

The surface the client wants the app to "lean into." Adherence + cohort benchmark + miss-reason diagnostics.

Blocks
Key stats (this month % / streak / longest) · Cohort benchmark (You · Median · Top quartile) with outcome link · Calendar heatmap · Miss reasons.
Entry points
Dashboard ring tap. (No dedicated bottom-nav tab.)
Main app · /profile

10 · Profile

Identity + cohort + medication history + settings. "Edit cohort filters" is the most important affordance — it changes the cohort used everywhere.

Blocks
Identity header · Your matched cohort card · Demographics rows (editable) · Medications (Current / Past) · Settings list · Export · Delete.
Chrome
Lōkahi logo at the footer above version/copyright.
Logging · /log-dose

11 · Log Dose

Fast data-capture for a taken dose. Optimized for one-handed thumb flow. Includes an optional check-in that updates baseline ratings.

Core fields
Date · Time · Injection site · Dose-changed toggle · Notes.
Optional check-in (collapsed by default)
Expand reveals 6 compact factor rows (Energy, Appetite, Mood, Sleep, Activity, Digestion) each with 5 buttons. "N of 6 rated" counter + "Clear ratings" link. On submit, these refresh the Baseline → now grid on Dashboard and Insights.
Submit
"Record dose" → success state → auto-return to /dashboard (~1.2s).
Logging · /log-side-effect

12 · Log Side Effect

Captures a single event with enough granularity to feed Insights trend cards and Comparison's side-effect-days-per-month outcome.

Fields
Category (10 chips) · Severity 1–5 · Duration (5 buckets) · Impact (4 buckets) · Date · Time.
Logging · /log-cost

13 · Log Cost

Tracks actual spend. Cohort context is at the top so the user immediately sees where they sit vs their peers.

Fields
Amount · Cost type (Copay / OOP / Coupon / Retail) · Pharmacy · Insurance applied · Supply duration · Fill date.
Logging · /switch-medication

14 · Switch Medication

Records a regimen change while retaining prior drug data for historical comparison.

Fields
New drug · Reason chips (multi-select) · Decision-maker · Switch date.
Disclaimer
Info card: "Informational only. Discuss changes with your prescriber."
Main app · /notifications

15 · Notifications

Passive surface for reminders, cohort insights, and confirmations.

Entry types
Reminder · Cohort insight · Weekly summary · Confirmation.
Entry point
Dashboard bell (not in bottom nav).
Cross-cutting

Patterns used across screens

CohortBadge

Reusable "YOUR MATCHED COHORT · n=1,247" strip with filter chips. Appears at the top of Comparison, Insights, and (compact) Adherence. Single source of truth for cohort state.

SectionHeader

Uppercase eyebrow + title + optional right-aligned meta (e.g., de-identified, 12 wks). Signals the clinical/official tone on every main-app card.

Return-to-dashboard on submit

All four log screens render a brief success confirmation and auto-navigate back to /dashboard so the user never has to back-button out of a log entry.

Category-aware dashboard

Today's eyebrow reads "TRIAL WEAVE · GLP-1". When new categories ship, the eyebrow and the current-regimen card swap; the rest of the chrome is unchanged.
